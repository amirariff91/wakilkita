# Impeccable Design Context — WakilKita

## Project
WakilKita is a trust-first constituency civic intake product. It helps residents nominate credible local representatives, submit local priorities, and later give one verified private support signal. It is not online voting, not SPR-affiliated, not a party tool, and not official results.

## Emotional goal
Serious, civic, calm, accountable. It should feel like public infrastructure: credible enough for NGOs/media, simple enough for normal residents, and careful about privacy.

## Page direction
- The page must feel like a resident action interface, not a pitch deck, policy memo, or civic dashboard.
- Core intake must appear before diagrams, generated visuals, and constituency data explorers.
- Public copy should lead with nominate, representative profile, private intake, issue priority, support signal, verified backing, and participant signal.
- Do not use founder-facing terms like “MVP” in the hero or primary CTA.
- Do not use “secure” unless the implementation provides and explains a real security guarantee.
- Clearly separate what is live now (private intake) from what comes later (verified support signal and aggregate public output).

## Visual system
- Palette: deep ink #071613, civic green #0F6B4D, soft mint #DDF7E8, warm parchment #F7F0E4, audit amber #D99A1E, slate #33413D.
- Typography: editorial serif-style headings via Georgia fallback; clean system sans body.
- Shape: rounded civic panels, map/grid motifs, transparent audit cards.
- Motion: minimal; trust-sensitive UI should not feel gimmicky.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- No generic startup hero with vague gradients.
- No crypto/blockchain theatre.
- No public-facing claims of official civic authority.
- No ballot imagery, party/campaign styling, government seals, or fake live counts.
- No decorative dashboards without clear trust meaning.
- No individual political preference exposure.
- No thick left-accent civic cards, nested card piles, or template feature grids.
